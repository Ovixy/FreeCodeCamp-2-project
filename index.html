Julius Caesar

The last Roman Dictator

Here's a timeline of Julius Caesar

  • 2 Jul 100 BCE - Gaius Julius Caesar is born, later becoming the founder of the Roman Empire.
  • 65 BCE - Julius Caesar is elected as aedile curule in Rome.
  • 60 BCE - 53 BCE - First Triumvirate' is created between Caesar, Pompey and Crassus.
  • 58 BCE - Caesar attacks and defeats the Helvetii.
  • 58 BCE - Julius Caesar invades Gaul. Rome’s first influence on European tribes begins.
  • 57 BCE - One of Caesar’s Roman armies defeats an army of Nervii, Atrebates, and Viromandui.
  • 55 BCE - 54 BCE - Julius Caesar invades Britain twice, and is successful on the second attempt, but soon withdraws to Gaul.
  • 54 BCE - 53 BCE - Ambiorix of the Eburones tribe destroys around 9,000 Roman soldiers at Atuatuca (modern day Belgium).
  • 54 BCE - 29 BCE - The Forum of Caesar is designed and constructed in Rome by Julius Caesar as another area to carry out judicial business.
  • 52 BCE - Julius Caesar is defeated at Gergovia by Vercingetorix.
  • 52 BCE - Vercingetorix eventually surrenders to Caesar's armies.
  • 49 BCE - Caesar crosses the Rubicon. The civil war between Caesar and Pompey begins.
  • 48 BCE - Caesar defeats Pompey (battle of Pharsalus); Pompey flees to Egypt and is killed by courtiers of Ptolemy XIII.
  • 23 Jun 47 BCE - Birth of Cleopatra's son, named Caesarion; it is thought Caesar was his father.
  • 46 BCE - Julius Caesar celebrates a triple triumph in Rome.
  • 44 BCE - Senators rebel and Julius Caesar is murdered in the senate
